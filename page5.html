Gas Giants(Jupiter & Saturn)

Gas Giants are planets that are mostly made out of gas, not very dense but a lot of mass.

Gas giants don't have a surface but more likely to have liquid Ocean of different elements.

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system with gravity 3 times stronger than on earth.

Jupiter helps earth by consuming asteroids that try to go to earth. 79 moons Orbit Jupiter and

the moon called Europa is known to have “life” on it. It has the mass of two and a half of all


planets in our solar system. There are constant storms on Jupiter full of lightning and

strong winds. Sometimes Jupiter is called a failed star or a brown dwarf because it does

not have enough materials to fuse into a star. The storm on Jupiter called the great Red spot,

has been around for 300 years and can fit 3 earths inside of it. It's shrinking slowly.

Mostly hydrogen and helium is in the atmosphere of jupiter. The atmosphere pressure turns

the gas into liquids

a half times that of Earth. It only has one-eighth the average density of Earth, being the least

dense planet. The planet is actulay buoyant on water. However, with its larger volume, Saturn

is over 95 times more massive. The radius of the rings on Saturn is 46390 miles which is the farthest

ring from the planet. The Rings of saturn are suprisingly thin and made out of small pieces of ice


and rocks. The 82 moons of saturn sometimes collide with the rings but are too small to disturb it.
