Ice Giants(Uranus & Neptune)

An ice giant is a giant planet composed mainly of elements heavier than hydrogen and

helium, such as oxygen, carbon, nitrogen, and sulfur. There are two ice giants in the solar

System: Uranus and Neptune. Uranus is 1.8 billion miles away from the sun is instead placed

like a wheel on a car instead of flat on the ground. It is is the 3rd least dense planet in the

solar system. The average temperature on Uranus is -357 degrees Fahrenheit with the length


of one year is 84 earth years.there are 27 moons orbiting Saturn with it's thin ring.

The core of Uranus is full of molten rock and the surface full of water and ammonia

and a thick layer atmosphere of hydrogen and helium and other trace elements.

Uranus is the coldest planet in the Solar System, with it's lowest temperatures

rescorded is is -371 degrees farenheiht. Seasons on Uranus is 42 years and to

put that into perspective an earth season lasts 4-3 years.

Neptune is The 8th and the last planet from the sun and has only made a few full orbits

around the sun. Neptune is an ice giant and has winds up to 1200 mph and no surface like the

Gas Giants. The average temperature is -350 degrees Fahrenheit and the rocky iron core is

the same temperature of the surface of the sun. The heat creates violent winds and hurricanes.

An example is The Great Dark Spot which can fit earth inside of it. The frozen clouds of

Neptune illuminate the surface of it's largest moon, Triton. Neptune was discovered by


calculation and not instead of observations. Neptune is very much like Uranus. It takes

4 hours for the light from the sun to reach Neptune. 15 moons are known to orbit Neptune

With it's very thin and small rings. Neptune is the smallest Gas Giant. Neptune also is

the smallest gas and ice giant. Neptune spins on it's axis faster than any other planet.
