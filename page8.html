Kuiper Belt and Dwarf Planets



Dwarf planets are planets that were reclassified into another type of planet.

There are three qualities for a planet to be a planet. One is that it has to

orbit a star. Second is that it has to have enough gravitational force to make

a spherical shape. Third It must be big enough that its gravity cleared away

any other objects of a similar size near its orbit around the Sun. Most of the

Dwarf planets fail on the third rule, listing the planets above.

The Kuiper belt is a thick layer of asteroids and comets that surround the solar

system. Beyond that is the Oort cloud which is where the gravitational pull of the

ends. This can be found trillions of miles from the center of the solar system.
